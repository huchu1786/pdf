By LovePDFs Team
Extract Pages from PDF Tutorial Guide

You need page 12 and page 34 from a 60-page PDF report — but not the pages between them. Or a colleague needs just the executive summary (pages 1-3) and the appendix (pages 55-60) from your annual report. The Extract Pages tool on PDF For All lets you cherry-pick any combination of pages from a PDF, instantly, free, browser-based.

What is Extract Pages from PDF?

Extract Pages from PDF lets you select specific pages — by individual number or non-continuous ranges — and save them as a new, smaller PDF. Unlike Split PDF which divides at fixed intervals, Extract Pages gives you complete control over exactly which pages appear in the output. Try Extract Pages from PDF free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need Extract Pages from PDF (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Sharing specific sections: Share only relevant pages with different stakeholders rather than the entire document.
  • Creating custom handouts: Extract key pages from a textbook or manual for targeted study or training material.
  • Legal document preparation: Extract only the relevant clauses or exhibits from a large agreement to share with a specific party.
  • Submission excerpts: Submit only specified pages from a larger report to an examiner or government portal.
  • Removing confidential pages: Extract all pages except confidential ones to create a shareable version.

How to Use Extract Pages from PDF on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the Extract Pages from PDF tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the Extract Pages tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Enter the page numbers you want to extract (e.g. 1, 3, 7-10, 25).
  4. Click Extract Pages.
  5. Download the new PDF containing only your selected pages.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use this after Merge PDF to refine a merged document — merge everything first, then extract the final selection.
  • For sensitive documents, use Remove Pages or Extract Pages to create a safe-to-share version.
  • After extracting, compress with Compress PDF if the result is still large.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing Extract Pages with Split PDF — Split divides at regular intervals, Extract picks any specific pages.
  • Not verifying the extracted PDF before sending — always open and review the output.
  • Extracting in the wrong order — the output preserves the order of the original document, not your input order.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I extract non-consecutive pages?

Yes. Enter page numbers and ranges in any combination — e.g. 1, 5, 12-15, 30.

Is the output in the same order as the original?

Yes. Pages appear in their original document order, regardless of the order you list them.

Can I extract from a locked PDF?

Unlock it first using the Unlock PDF tool.

Does extracting reduce quality?

No. Extracted pages retain original image and text quality.
