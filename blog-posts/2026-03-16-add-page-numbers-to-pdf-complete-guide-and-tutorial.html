Add Page Numbers to PDF: Complete Guide and Tutorial

By LovePDFs Team
Add Page Numbers to PDF Tutorial Guide

You merged a 40-page project report from three different sources and now the page numbers jump all over the place — or there are no page numbers at all. Your professor or client is asking you to reference specific pages. The Add Page Numbers tool on PDF For All stamps consistent numbering across your entire PDF instantly, for free.

What is Add Page Numbers?

Add Page Numbers is a free browser-based tool that inserts page numbers into every page of a PDF. Choose the position (header or footer, left or right or centre), the font, the number format, and whether to start numbering from a specific number (useful when merging documents into a larger file). Try Add Page Numbers free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need Add Page Numbers (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Merged documents: After combining multiple PDFs with Merge PDF, add consistent sequential page numbers across the whole document.
  • Academic reports: Add page numbers to thesis chapters, project reports, and seminar papers for professional presentation.
  • Legal and corporate documents: Numbered pages make references and cross-references unambiguous in contracts and agreements.
  • Presentations converted to PDF: Add page numbers to slide decks converted to PDF for easier navigation during client meetings.
  • Court and government filings: Many court submissions and official reports require numbered pages.

How to Use Add Page Numbers on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the Add Page Numbers tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the Add Page Numbers tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Choose your position: top-left, top-centre, top-right, bottom-left, bottom-centre, or bottom-right.
  4. Set your starting number, font size, and format (e.g. 1, 2, 3 or Page 1 of N).
  5. Click Add Numbers and download your numbered PDF.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Set the starting number to match the document's position in a larger publication if combining later with Merge PDF.
  • Footer-centre is the most universally accepted position for academic and professional documents.
  • Add a header and footer with section names using Add Header & Footer in addition to page numbers.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Adding page numbers before merging documents — merge first, then number the combined file.
  • Using a font size that is too large and overlaps body text — 9–11pt is the standard range.
  • Not checking whether the numbers appear on the correct position on every page including the last.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I start numbering from a page other than 1?

Yes. You can set any starting number, for example if the document is a chapter in a larger work.

Can I add Roman numerals for front matter?

Yes. The tool supports multiple number formats including Roman numerals.

Does this work on protected PDFs?

Unlock them first with Unlock PDF before adding page numbers.

Can I remove page numbers I already added?

Not directly — keep a backup of the unnumbered original before adding numbers.
