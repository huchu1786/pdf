By LovePDFs Team
Convert PDF to Excel Tutorial Guide

You have a bank statement or a government report as a PDF, and you need the numbers in a spreadsheet to run calculations or create charts. Retyping columns of data from a PDF into Excel wastes hours and invites errors. The PDF to Excel tool on PDF For All extracts those tables automatically — free, in your browser, no upload required.

What is PDF to Excel?

PDF to Excel is a free browser-based converter that extracts tables and tabular data from PDF documents into a CSV format compatible with Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, and LibreOffice Calc. It saves hours of manual data entry and eliminates transcription errors. Try PDF to Excel free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need PDF to Excel (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Bank statement analysis: Extract transaction tables to create expense reports and pivot tables in Excel.
  • GST and tax filing: Pull figures from tax PDFs into a spreadsheet for calculations.
  • Government tender data: Extract project tables from government PDF notifications into Excel for bid analysis.
  • Academic research: Convert research data tables from PDF papers into Excel for statistical analysis.
  • Inventory and purchases: Extract purchase order or inventory tables from PDF invoices.

How to Use PDF to Excel on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the PDF to Excel tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the PDF to Excel tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload your PDF file.
  3. Click Convert to CSV. The tool extracts tables from your PDF.
  4. Download the CSV file and open it in Excel or Google Sheets.
  5. Adjust column widths and formatting as needed.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • For scanned PDFs, run OCR PDF first to make table data recognisable.
  • Clean, well-structured tables in digital PDFs extract best.
  • Use Google Sheets for free if you don't have Excel — import the CSV directly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Expecting complex merged cells to extract perfectly — some manual cleanup is needed.
  • Using on scanned tables without running OCR first.
  • Not verifying extracted figures against the original PDF — always spot-check.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will all tables extract correctly?

Standard well-structured tables extract well. Complex merged cells may need manual adjustment.

Is this the same as PDF to CSV?

Yes. CSV (comma-separated values) opens directly in Excel and is the standard intermediate format.

Can it extract data from scanned PDFs?

Run OCR PDF on the scanned document first, then convert.

Is there a row limit?

No. The tool handles large tables across many pages.
