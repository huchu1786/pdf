By LovePDFs Team
Extract Images from PDF Tutorial Guide

A client PDF contains beautiful product photographs that you need for your website, or a report has charts you want to reuse in a presentation. Right-clicking and saving from a PDF reader gives you a low-resolution screenshot. The Extract Images from PDF tool on PDF For All saves all embedded images at their original resolution — free, browser-based, instant.

What is Extract Images from PDF?

Extract Images from PDF detects and extracts all raster images embedded inside a PDF document, saving them as individual JPG or PNG files. You get every image at its original embedded resolution — far better quality than screenshotting from a PDF reader. Try Extract Images from PDF free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need Extract Images from PDF (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Product imagery: Extract product photos from catalogue PDFs for use on e-commerce listings.
  • Presentation graphics: Pull charts and diagrams from PDF reports to embed in PowerPoint presentations.
  • Website content: Extract images from brochure PDFs to publish on your company website.
  • Social media assets: Extract infographic images from PDF reports for sharing on LinkedIn or Instagram.
  • Archive recovery: Recover images from PDFs when the original source files are unavailable.

How to Use Extract Images from PDF on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the Extract Images from PDF tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the Extract Images from PDF tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Click Extract Images.
  4. Preview the extracted images and download them individually or as a ZIP archive.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Choose PNG for images with text or transparency; JPG for photographs.
  • After extracting, compress large images with Compress Image before uploading to a website.
  • Resize extracted images with Resize Image to match your website or social media dimensions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Expecting screenshots embedded as images to be high-resolution — the quality is capped at the original.
  • Downloading all images without reviewing which are relevant — some PDFs embed dozens of small UI elements.
  • Not compressing extracted images before web upload — large images slow down websites.

Frequently Asked Questions

What formats are the extracted images saved in?

JPG or PNG depending on your selection. The tool extracts images at their embedded resolution.

Can I extract images from a scanned PDF?

Scanned PDFs are images of pages. Use PDF to JPG instead to convert each scanned page to an image file.

Will the image quality be the same as the original?

Yes. Embedded images are extracted at their original resolution without recompression.

Is there a limit on how many images I can extract?

No. All images embedded across all pages are extracted in a single operation.
