Remove Annotations: Complete Guide and Tutorial

By LovePDFs Team
Remove Annotations Tutorial Guide

You received a heavily marked-up PDF back from a reviewer — covered in highlights, sticky notes, and comment bubbles. You incorporated all the feedback and now need to share a clean, annotation-free final version. The Remove Annotations tool on PDF For All strips all marks from a PDF in one click, free, browser-based.

What is Remove Annotations?

Remove Annotations deletes all annotation layers from a PDF — highlights, comments, sticky notes, stamps, drawings, and markup added in review tools. The underlying page content is untouched. The cleaned PDF looks exactly as it did before the annotations were added. Try Remove Annotations free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need Remove Annotations (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Finalising reviewed documents: Remove all reviewer comments before sharing the approved final version.
  • Cleaning up textbook PDFs: Strip excessive previous-student highlights from shared study PDFs.
  • Preparing client deliverables: Remove internal review notes and revision marks before client delivery.
  • Archiving clean versions: Store annotation-free master copies alongside annotated working copies.
  • Printing without marks: Remove distracting annotations before printing a clean copy.

How to Use Remove Annotations on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the Remove Annotations tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the Remove Annotations tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload your annotated PDF.
  3. Click Remove Annotations.
  4. Download the clean PDF and open it to verify all marks are removed.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • After removing, flatten with Flatten PDF to prevent any residual layers.
  • If annotations in a signed document need to stay, remove only comment-type annotations and keep signature fields.
  • Always check the final document page by page to confirm all marks are gone.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Removing annotations before incorporating feedback — process all changes first, then clean.
  • Confusing annotations with form field content — form responses are separate from annotations.
  • Not backing up the annotated original — once removed, annotations cannot be recovered.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are form field values removed too?

No. Form field values are separate from annotations. Use Flatten PDF to embed form values, not remove annotations.

Will the tool remove all annotation types?

Yes — highlights, comments, sticky notes, stamps, ink drawings, and text markups are all removed.

Will removing annotations change the page layout?

No. Only the overlay layers are removed. The underlying page content is unchanged.

Can I selectively remove specific annotations?

For selective removal, use Edit PDF and manually erase specific marks.
