Add Watermark to PDF: Complete Guide and Tutorial

By LovePDFs Team
Add Watermark to PDF Tutorial Guide

You are sharing a client proposal and want to stamp DRAFT or CONFIDENTIAL across every page before sending — without having to open Photoshop or pay for Adobe. Or you are distributing a training PDF and want your company name on every page. The Add Watermark tool on PDF For All does this in seconds, free, in any browser.

What is Add Watermark?

Add Watermark is a free browser-based tool that stamps text or image watermarks across every page of a PDF. Choose the text, font size, colour, opacity, and position. Watermarks are embedded permanently — viewers cannot remove them without the original tool. All processing is local. Try Add Watermark free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need Add Watermark (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Draft and review documents: Add a large diagonal DRAFT watermark to documents that are not yet finalised.
  • Confidential documents: Mark sensitive financial or legal PDFs with CONFIDENTIAL before sharing across departments.
  • Branded deliverables: Stamp your company name and logo on client proposals, reports, and presentations.
  • Sample portfolios: Add a SAMPLE watermark to portfolio PDFs shared with potential clients before payment.
  • Educational handouts: Watermark course material PDFs with your institute's name to prevent unauthorised redistribution.

How to Use Add Watermark on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the Add Watermark tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the Add Watermark tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload your PDF file.
  3. Type your watermark text (e.g. DRAFT, CONFIDENTIAL, or your company name).
  4. Adjust font size, opacity, colour, rotation angle, and position.
  5. Click Add Watermark and download the stamped PDF.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Set opacity to 20–35% for a professional look that does not obscure content.
  • Use diagonal rotation (45°) for maximum visibility across the page.
  • For image watermarks (logos), use a PNG with a transparent background.
  • After watermarking, compress the file if its size increased using Compress PDF.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Setting opacity too high — watermarks that cover content make documents unreadable.
  • Placing a watermark only on the first page — it should appear on every page of the document.
  • Using bright colours that clash with black-body text and harm readability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can recipients remove the watermark?

Not without the original unwatermarked file or a dedicated PDF editing tool. For higher security, combine with Protect PDF.

Can I watermark every page automatically?

Yes — the watermark is applied to all pages by default.

Can I add a logo image instead of text?

Yes. Upload a PNG image as your watermark for image-based branding.

Can I remove a watermark I added?

Not directly once it is baked into the PDF. Keep a backup of the unwatermarked original.
