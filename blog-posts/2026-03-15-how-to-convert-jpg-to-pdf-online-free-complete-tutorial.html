How to Convert JPG to PDF Online Free

Everything from single-image conversions to turning a full album of photos into one clean PDF — right in your browser, no account needed.

March 4, 2026 • 7 min read
By LovePDFs Team

You have photos — from a phone camera, a scanner, a design app — and you need to share them in PDF format. Maybe a portal accepts only PDFs, or a client expects a professional-looking file rather than a heap of image attachments. Converting JPG to PDF is the right move, and this tutorial shows you exactly how to do it efficiently.

Why convert images to PDF?

PDF is a universal format that displays consistently on every device and operating system. Here's why people convert JPG images to PDF regularly:

  • Government and academic submissions almost universally require a single PDF, not separate image files. Scanned identity documents, mark sheets, and certificates frequently need combining into one PDF for online portals.
  • Professional presentations — real estate agents create PDF property walkthroughs from listing photos; photographers share proofs as PDFs instead of dumps of images.
  • Archiving handwritten notes. Students who photograph lecture notes or handwritten assignments on their phones can convert multiple photos into a single ordered PDF that's easier to search, annotate, and store.
  • Document scanning workflows. Flatbed scanners sometimes save individual scans as JPEG files. Converting them to a single PDF creates a proper digital document.

JPG vs PNG vs WebP — does the input format matter?

The LovePDFs JPG to PDF converter accepts JPG, PNG, and WebP images — so whatever format your photos are in, you're covered. The converter embeds each image at its original resolution, so image quality in the PDF output matches the source photo.

One thing to know: JPG images use lossy compression, meaning they may have slight artifacts around edges compared to PNG. If you need the absolute sharpest rendering (for example, diagrams with fine text), take your screenshots in PNG format before converting.

How to convert JPG images to PDF step by step

  1. Open the LovePDFs JPG to PDF converter.
  2. Click Choose Images or drag your files into the drop zone. You can upload a single image or multiple images at once.
  3. If you uploaded multiple images, drag the thumbnails to arrange them in the order you want. Each image will become one page in the final PDF, from top to bottom.
  4. Choose your page settings: A4 is standard for most document workflows; Letter is the US equivalent; Fit to image creates a page exactly the size of each image with no borders.
  5. Select orientation — Portrait for vertical images (typical photos), Landscape for wide panoramic shots or presentations.
  6. Click Convert to PDF. The PDF is generated entirely in your browser — your images are never uploaded to any server.
  7. Download the resulting PDF file.

Tips for better conversion results

  • Improve image quality before converting. Blurry or dark photos make a poor PDF. If your scanned images are skewed, use a scanner app to deskew them before converting.
  • Compress the output PDF if needed. A PDF made from 20 high-resolution photos can easily be 40–80 MB. After converting, run the PDF through the Compress PDF tool to bring the size down to email-friendly levels.
  • Add page numbers after converting. Multi-page PDFs created from photos often need page numbers for reference. Use Add Page Numbers after conversion.
  • Rename your images in order before uploading. If you have 15 images named IMG_001.jpg through IMG_015.jpg, they'll automatically load in the right sequence. Random filenames from messaging apps often result in a jumbled order that requires manual drag-and-drop resorting.

Frequently asked questions

Can I convert more than one image at a time?

Yes. You can upload as many images as you need. Each image becomes one page in the output PDF, in the order you arrange them.

Is the image quality reduced during conversion?

No — images are embedded at their original resolution. The converter does not downsample or recompress images by default.

My output PDF is very large. What can I do?

Run the resulting PDF through the Compress PDF tool. For most photo-based PDFs, this reduces file size by 50–75% with minimal visible quality change.

Does this work on iPhone?

Yes. The converter works in Safari on iOS and Chrome on Android without requiring any app download. You can even convert photos directly from your phone's camera roll.
