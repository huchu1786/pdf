By LovePDFs Team
Compare PDF Tutorial Guide

Your client sent back an updated version of the contract and claims only one clause was changed — but you need to verify. Manually reading two 30-page PDFs side-by-side to find differences takes hours and leaves room for error. The Compare PDF tool on PDF For All highlights every difference between two documents instantly, free, without sending your files to any server.

What is Compare PDF?

Compare PDF is a free browser-based tool that places two PDF documents side by side and visually highlights all differences — added text, deleted text, moved content, and changed sections. Ideal for contract review, document versioning, and proofreading. Runs entirely in your browser. Try Compare PDF free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need Compare PDF (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Contract version control: Verify exactly what changed between draft 1 and draft 2 of a legal agreement before signing.
  • Academic proofreading: Compare a submitted assignment with a revised version to confirm all corrections were applied.
  • Regulatory compliance: Ensure a final compliance form matches an approved template before submission.
  • Policy updates: Compare new and old company policy documents to identify newly added or removed clauses.
  • Publishing review: Proofread a final PDF against the edited draft to confirm all changes from editorial review are incorporated.

How to Use Compare PDF on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the Compare PDF tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the Compare PDF tool on PDF For All.
  2. Upload the original PDF on the left panel.
  3. Upload the revised PDF on the right panel.
  4. Click Compare — differences are highlighted in both documents.
  5. Review the highlighted differences and note all changes.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Compare text-based PDFs for best results — for scanned comparisons, run OCR PDF on both files first.
  • For very long documents, use Split PDF to compare section-by-section.
  • Take a screenshot or print the comparison result before closing the tab for your records.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Comparing a digital and a scanned version directly — run OCR on the scanned one first.
  • Forgetting to check every page of the comparison — scroll through all pages before concluding.
  • Trusting a comparison of files where one has been reprinted and re-scanned — pixel comparison will show differences everywhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it compare scanned PDFs?

For best results, both PDFs should be digital or OCR-processed. Comparing scanned images may produce less precise results.

Can I export the comparison result?

Take a screenshot of the highlighted comparison view. Exporting as a separate file is not currently available.

Does it detect moved paragraphs?

It highlights content differences including moved blocks, though detection depends on the PDF structure.

Is this free?

Yes, completely free with no account required and no files uploaded to any server.
