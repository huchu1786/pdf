By LovePDFs Team
OCR / Extract Text from PDF Tutorial Guide

You scanned your Aadhaar card or old marksheets and now you need to pull the address or marks into a form — but selecting the text just selects the whole image. Scanned PDFs contain pictures of text, not actual selectable characters. The OCR PDF tool on PDF For All converts those images into real, searchable, selectable text — free, in your browser.

What is OCR PDF?

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) PDF is a free browser-based tool that analyses pages of a scanned PDF and recognises the text within them, creating a new version where the text is fully selectable, searchable, and copy-able. No installation, no account, and no upload to any server. Try OCR PDF free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need OCR PDF (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Scanned Aadhaar and PAN cards: Make the text in scanned ID documents selectable so you can copy your address or ID number.
  • Old marksheets and certificates: Convert scanned degree or school certificates into searchable PDFs for digital records.
  • Government circulars: Unlock the text in scanned government circulars and notifications for quoting in official letters.
  • Legal and historical documents: Turn scanned contracts, deeds, or archival documents into searchable, quotable PDFs.
  • Research papers: Make scanned academic papers searchable for keyword search and citation extraction.

How to Use OCR PDF on PDF For All — Step by Step

Using the OCR PDF tool is quick and requires no account or download:

  1. Open the OCR PDF tool on PDF For All — no account needed.
  2. Upload your scanned PDF.
  3. Select the language of the document for better recognition accuracy.
  4. Click Recognise Text. OCR runs in your browser using WebAssembly.
  5. Download the new PDF with selectable, searchable text.

All processing happens locally in your browser. Your files are never uploaded to any server.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Clean, well-lit scans produce far better OCR results — blurry or dark scans result in errors.
  • Deskew tilted scans before running OCR with Deskew PDF — straight text is easier to recognise.
  • After OCR, convert to Word using PDF to Word for full editing capability.
  • OCR works on Hindi and regional language scans too if the correct language is selected.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Running OCR on an already-digital PDF — it is not needed; text is already selectable.
  • Selecting the wrong language, which will produce garbled output.
  • Expecting 100% accuracy on low-quality, handwritten, or noisy scans.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the OCR?

For clean, high-resolution document scans, accuracy is typically above 95%. For poor-quality or handwritten text, results will vary.

Does it work on Hindi documents?

Yes, select Hindi as the document language before processing for better results.

Can I OCR then edit the text in Word?

Yes. After OCR, use PDF to Word and you will get an editable Word document with the recognised text.

Is my scanned Aadhaar safe?

Completely. All processing is local — your document never leaves your device.
