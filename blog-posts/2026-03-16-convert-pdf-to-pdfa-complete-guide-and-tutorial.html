Your legal team, archiving system, or government portal is asking for your documents in PDF/A format and you are not sure what that means or how to create one. What is PDF to PDF/A?

PDF/A is an ISO-standardised version of PDF designed specifically for long-term digital archiving. Unlike regular PDFs, PDF/A files are self-contained — all fonts, colour profiles, and metadata are embedded. PDF to PDF/A converts your regular PDF into this archive-safe format for permanent, reliable storage. Try PDF to PDF/A free here — no signup, no installation, works on mobile.

Why You Need PDF to PDF/A (Real-World Use Cases)

  • Court and legal filings: Many Indian courts and legal systems require PDF/A for electronic filings.
  • Government and public records: National and state archives require PDF/A for long-term document preservation.
  • Financial and audit records: Convert financial statements and audit reports to PDF/A for compliant record-keeping.
  • Academic and research submissions: Some journals and institutional repositories require PDF/A for thesis and paper submissions.
  • Corporate archiving: Convert HR records, contracts, and correspondence to PDF/A for ISO-compliant document management.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between PDF and PDF/A?

PDF/A is a restricted, self-contained version designed for archiving. It embeds all fonts and resources internally and does not allow encryption or external content.

Which PDF/A version should I use?

PDF/A-1b is the most widely required. Check with your archiving system or legal portal.

Can I convert a scanned PDF to PDF/A?

Yes, but running OCR PDF first is recommended for searchable archival quality.

Is the converted file suitable for courts?

PDF/A is the accepted standard for many legal electronic filings. Verify requirements with your specific court.
