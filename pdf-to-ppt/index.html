Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert a PDF to PowerPoint without Adobe?
Yes — this tool requires no Adobe software or account. It runs entirely in your browser and converts the PDF directly to a .pptx file you can open in Microsoft PowerPoint, Google Slides, or any compatible software.
Will the formatting and layout be preserved?
Text blocks, headings, and images are extracted and placed into slides as accurately as possible. Complex multi-column layouts or custom fonts may not be reproduced exactly, but the content will be fully editable. Enable "Preserve PDF layout" in options for best results with layout-heavy PDFs.
Are my files safe? Are they uploaded to a server?
Your files are never uploaded anywhere. All processing happens inside your browser using PDF.js and pptxgenjs. Your documents stay completely private on your device — no account, no server, no data stored.
What is the maximum file size I can convert?
Since conversion runs in your browser, the limit depends on your device's memory. PDFs up to 50MB (typically 200+ pages) convert reliably on most modern devices. For very large files, we recommend splitting the PDF first using our Split PDF tool.
Can I convert a scanned PDF to PowerPoint?
Scanned PDFs are image-based and don't contain extractable text. For best results with scanned documents, use our OCR tool first to extract text, then convert to PowerPoint. The tool will still extract images from scanned PDFs as image slides.
What formats can I open the PPTX file in?
The output .pptx file is fully compatible with Microsoft PowerPoint (all versions), Google Slides (upload via Drive), LibreOffice Impress, Apple Keynote (with import), and WPS Office.
Does it support multi-column PDF layouts?
Basic multi-column layouts are detected and split into text blocks per slide. Enable "Preserve PDF layout" in options to get closer positioning. For complex magazine-style layouts, results vary depending on how the original PDF was created.
Will slide transitions be included?
No. PDF files do not store transition data — they are static page-based documents. If the original presentation had transitions, those were lost when it was saved as a PDF. You can add new transitions manually in PowerPoint after conversion.
